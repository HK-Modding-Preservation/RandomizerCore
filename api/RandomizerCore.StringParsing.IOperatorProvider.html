Remarks

The "binding power" concept was borrowed from this explainer of Pratt Parsing: https://matklad.github.io/2020/04/13/simple-but-powerful-pratt-parsing.html. It is essentially an alternative way to think about precedence, but gives a natural way to explain biased associativity.

In short, each operator has a distinct left and right binding power. When looking at B in the expression A &lt;op1> B &lt;op2> C, you see whether the right BP of op1 is higher or lower than the left BP of op2 to see which operator has the stronger ability to "pull" or "bind" B into its expression. Left and right BP can be (and should be) asymmetric to give a well-defined preference for the case where op1 and op2 are the same operator (or 2 operators at the same precedence level).
